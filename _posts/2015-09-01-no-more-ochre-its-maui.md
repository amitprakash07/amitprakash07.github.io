**Ochre** was the game we had been building since the second semester. The pitch was **cultural storytelling** through unusual mechanics—especially **augmented reality** woven into the narrative.

Last semester we implemented a lot of AR-driven gameplay using the **Metaio** SDK. Metaio was straightforward to work with and, thanks to **Unity** integration, supported almost every platform we cared about.

![Development screenshot]({{ '/assets/img/blog/2015/maui-ochre/maui-dev-screenshot.png' | relative_url }})

Right before **Fall** started, we learned that **Apple had acquired Metaio**. Support and licensing for the SDK went away.

After long **email threads with faculty** and team meetings, we kept the **core idea**: still **cultural narration + AR**. The hard part was that we had to **rebuild the tech from scratch**.

We moved to another AR SDK called **Max AR** and started over on tracking, rotation, and the AR mini-games. With solid summer work from the team, we had something to show on the **first Fall presentation**.

**Contrary to what we expected**, faculty still liked the direction—we hadn’t changed the fantasy, only the **technology stack**. We reframed the story around **Hawaiian culture and four gods**: a boy’s **journey toward the mountains** he can’t finish without earning the gods’ help. We leaned into **AR mini-game levels**, especially for **divine** encounters—AR felt like a stronger fit for **god** interactions than for ordinary human NPCs.
