This was the **last prototype** of the semester, and everyone wanted to bring their best. One change this round: we could **pick our own team**—and we formed the group for this project.

We could also build **whatever we wanted**, which sounds easy until you try to settle on an idea. We spent about **two hours** debating directions. Our producer **Cameron** proposed a game built around **electromagnetism**: mechanics based on how a **charged particle** behaves in a **magnetic field**, aligned with the **left-hand rule** (see [Fleming’s left-hand rule for motors](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fleming%27s_left-hand_rule_for_motors)).

### Left-hand rule (reference)

![Left-hand outline]({{ '/assets/img/blog/2014/conduit-electromag/left-hand-outline.png' | relative_url }})

After about **a week and a half**, we had a **basic build**; within **three weeks** we had a solid working version. The game looked strong thanks to art and **particle systems**. We still needed strong levels—**Cameron** and **Omey** each designed one—and we polished using **first-play feedback** from **Roger**.

### Team

![Team — Electromag]({{ '/assets/img/blog/2014/conduit-electromag/team-electromag.png' | relative_url }})

The highlight of this prototype was showing the game at the **EAE open house**. Feedback was positive, and many players especially liked the **core mechanics**.
