This was our **third prototype** after Fall Break. We were asked to design a **serious game**: a game meant to help people in an interactive way (through engagement and fun)—whether that means speeding up learning for students or supporting patient recovery. We had to deliver something in that spirit.

We used a **design box** process to explore ideas. After brainstorming, we landed on a game about challenges at **Ebola training camps**. At first, the goal was to help **aid workers** understand procedures at training centers.

After a week, we **reframed the problem**: instead of a tutorial for workers, we focused on issues tied to the **outbreak** itself. Our producer brought in expert input—**under-resourcing** (lack of money) was a major driver of the crisis. We pivoted to a **serious, strategy-style** game to surface that, and built it in **Unity**.
