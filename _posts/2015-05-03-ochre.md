**Ochre** was the game the **industry panel** selected to move forward as a **thesis** project. Its hook was **control**: the **webcam** was the only input—the player moved a **physical card**, and when the game recognized the card/template, it triggered the matching in-game behavior.

Early on we had a hard time picking the **tech stack**. We first tried **OpenCV**. It worked well for **object recognition**, but it wasn’t a great fit for the kind of **continuous tracking** we needed from the camera.

Our producer pointed us to **Metaio**, an SDK with stronger **tracking** support. After experimenting with it, we could **recognize and track** templated objects on the physical card.

> **Note:** Metaio was later acquired and its SDK is no longer maintained; this post reflects the **2015** toolchain. The same line of work later continued as **Maui**.
